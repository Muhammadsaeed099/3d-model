Methodology & honesty note

What's sourced, what's rounded, what's derived, and what's invented. Nothing here pretends to be more precise than it is.

About the numbers

Sales figures are approximate lifetime units sold, rounded to the nearest million, compiled from publicly reported publisher figures and aggregated sales trackers. Catalogue titles keep selling, so these are a ballpark snapshot (cutoff around 2025 to 2026) rather than an audited ledger. Strict ranking order is preserved with no ties.

What's what: provenance of every element

  • SourcedSales & ranking: from public reporting (publisher statements, annual reports, aggregate trackers).
  • RoundedSales numbers: rounded to the nearest million; treat exact figures as approximate.
  • DerivedPodium height: scaled linearly to the sales number. Tallest podium = #1.
  • Derived"Share of #1" bar in the detail panel: computed live from this dataset.
  • SourcedCover art: reference images supplied for each title. These are illustrative, not official box art from publishers.
  • InventedFlag art: drawn procedurally on canvas as a decorative cue for the publisher's home region. Not official heraldry.
  • InventedTrees, fence, banner, lighting: purely scenographic, no data meaning.
  • Per-title caveats (bundling, edition scope, etc.) appear in each exhibit's detail panel where applicable.

Controls reference

  • Drag (left mouse / one finger): orbit the camera. Full 360° horizontal and vertical range.
  • Right-click drag: pan the look-at target across the scene.
  • Scroll / two-finger slide: zoom in or out. Slide sideways to orbit. Hold Shift while scrolling to pan.
  • Pinch (trackpad): zoom in or out.
  • WASD / Arrow keys: walk through the museum. Q and E raise and lower the camera.
  • ◀ Prev: move to the previous-ranked exhibit (e.g. from #5 to #4).
  • Next ▶: move to the next-ranked exhibit (e.g. from #5 to #6).
  • Reset: return to the starting overview shot.
  • Jump to #: type a rank and press Enter (or click Go) to fly directly to that exhibit.
